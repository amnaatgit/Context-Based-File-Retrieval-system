# Performance Review Guide

## Purpose
This guide explains how managers and employees should prepare for and conduct performance reviews. It is an HR policy document owned by the People Success team and applies to all full-time staff.

## Review Cycle
Performance reviews run twice a year, in June and December. Managers open the review two weeks before the meeting so employees can complete a self-assessment.

## Rating Scale
1. Below expectations
2. Meets some expectations
3. Meets expectations
4. Exceeds expectations
5. Outstanding

## What to Prepare
Employees should gather examples of completed projects, goals achieved, and areas where they grew. Managers should review the previous cycle's goals and collect peer feedback where relevant.

## Meeting Structure
- Reflect on the past cycle and celebrate wins
- Discuss areas for development honestly and supportively
- Agree on three to five goals for the next cycle
- Confirm any training or support the employee needs

## Compensation Link
Ratings inform the annual compensation and promotion review handled by the HR manager, but a single review does not automatically change pay. Decisions consider the full year and the payroll budget.

## Confidentiality
Review notes are confidential HR records. They are stored securely and shared only with the employee, their manager, and authorized HR staff.
